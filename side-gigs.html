Playing Games with My Feet

I have a mechanical disability which makes it difficult to play video games with my hands, so I've been playing games with my feet since 2021. I use a set of Logitech Accessibility Kit buttons, switches and triggers, all conntcted to an Xbox Adaptive Controller. This is wired into a Magic NS adapter, which is also wirelessly synced to a Nintendo Switch Pro Controller to provide use of thumbsticks; wired joysticks could also plug directly into the XAC. This set-up works for Switch and PC games (and probably Xbox, which I don't have), and I use an additional XXX adapter to play games on PS5 (only works for PS4 games at the moment).

My buttons are normally velcro-mounted to some panels on a floor mat, but they can detach and reattach to any velcro surface. For certain games, I'll attach them to various sections of my chair or couch to incorporate my legs, elbows, or neck. My current set-up took around a year of trial and error, so I've tried various configurations, controllers and adapters. I'm always happy to discuss or field questions on custom controllers, accessibility featurres and gaming with disabilities.
